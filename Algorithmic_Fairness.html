AI & Fairness: Bias Mitigation¶

1 Objective:¶

Objective of this project is to mitigate bias in the Student Perfromance dataset and compare the fairness metrics on original and debiased datasets. First step is to remove the bias in the dataset and then use debiased dataset in downstream machine learning algorithms so that results of the algorithms preserve fairness.

The idea is that we want to learn a new representation Z such that it removes the information correlated to the sensitive attribute A and preserves the information of X as much as possible.[1]

2 Data Analysis & Data Preparation¶

2.1 Dataset¶

This dataset consists of data on student's achievement in secondary education of two Portuguese schools. The data attributes include student grades, demographic, social and school related features and it was collected by using school reports and questionnaires. Two datasets are provided regarding the performance in two distinct subjects: Mathematics (mat) and Portuguese language (por). These two datasets were modeled under binary/five-level classification and regression tasks. The target attribute G3 has a strong correlation with attributes G2 and G1. This occurs because G3 is the final year grade (issued at the 3rd period), while G1 and G2 correspond to the 1st and 2nd period grades. It is more difficult to predict G3 without G2 and G1, but such prediction is much more useful. For this project dataset for Portuguese language is used. [2] Dataset can be found at http://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Student+Performance

Depended Variables

  • G3 - final grade (numeric: from 0 to 20, output target)
  • absences - number of school absences (numeric: from 0 to 93)

Legally Recognized Protected Class and corresponding Legal Precedence/Law

  • sex - student's sex (Equal Pay Act of 1963; Civil Rights Act of 1964, 1991)
  • age - student's age (Age Discrimination in Employment Act of 1967)
  • Pstatus - parent's cohabitation status (Civil Rights Act of 1968)

2.5 Discretize Dependent Variables¶

To predict whether student is a high performer, dependent variable G3 is converted to binary attribute. If student's score is between 12 and 20 then student is marked as "Above Average Performer" and represented as 1. If the student perfromance is below 12 (0-11), then student is marked as "Below Average Performer" and represented as 0.

To predict student with high attendence, dependent variable "absences" is also converted to binary. If student has absences between 0 and 4, then student is marked "Focused" and marked as 1. If the student's absence is more than 4, then student is marked as "Unfocused" and represented as 0.

3 Bias Mitigation¶

To identify and mitigate the bais in the dataset, two fairness metrics Statistical Parity Difference and Disparate Impact are measured and bias in the dataset is mitigated by using Reweighing algorithm.

3.1 Unprivileged & Privileged Protected Class Members¶

Protected Class: Sex Unprivileged: Female Privileged: Male

Protected Class: Age Unprivileged: Teen Privileged: Adult

Protected Class: Pstatus Unprivileged: Apart Privileged: Together

3.2 Fairness Metrics¶

Statistical Parity Difference: Computed as the difference of the rate of favorable outcomes received by the unprivileged group to the privileged group. The ideal value of this metric is 0

Disparate Impact: Computed as the ratio of rate of favorable outcome for the unprivileged group to that of the privileged group. The ideal value of this metric is 1.0 A value < 1 implies higher benefit for the privileged group and a value >1 implies a higher benefit for the unprivileged group.

3.3 Pre-processing Algorithm¶

Reweighing: Use to mitgate bias in training data. Modifies the weights of different training examples.

def plot_DI(type, protectedvar, dependentvar, DIval): 
    fig, axs = plt.subplots();
    plt.bar([0,1,2], [0, DIval,0])
    plt.axhline(y=1, color='black')
    plt.axhline(y=0.5, linestyle='dashed', color='black', alpha=0.5)
    plt.plot(0)
    plt.ylim(0,1.5)
    plt.xlim(0,2)
    plt.axhspan(0.8, 1.2, facecolor='grey', alpha=0.5, zorder=-100, label="Fair")
    plt.axhspan(0, 0.8, facecolor='lightgrey', alpha=0.5, zorder=-100, label="Bias")
    plt.xticks([])
    plt.yticks([0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5])
    plt.title("{} - Protected: {}, Dependent: {}, Disparate Impact: {}".format(type, protectedvar,  dependentvar, DIval))
    plt.legend(loc='center left', bbox_to_anchor=(1, 0.5))
    fig.tight_layout()

def plot_SPD(type, protectedvar, dependentvar, SPDval): 
    fig, axs = plt.subplots();
    plt.bar([0,1,2], [0,SPDval,0])
    plt.axhline(y=0, color='black')
    plt.axhline(y=0.5, linestyle='dashed', color='black', alpha=0.5)
    plt.axhline(y=-0.5, linestyle='dashed', color='black', alpha=0.5)
    plt.plot(0)
    plt.ylim(-1,1)
    plt.xlim(0,2)
    plt.axhspan(-0.1, 0.1, facecolor='grey', alpha=0.5, zorder=-100, label="Fair")
    plt.axhspan(0.1, -1, facecolor='lightgrey', alpha=0.5, zorder=-100, label="Bias")
    plt.xticks([])
    plt.yticks([-1, -0.5, 0, 0.5, 1.0])
    plt.title("{} - Protected: {}, Dependent: {}, Statistical Parity: {}".format(type, protectedvar,  dependentvar, SPDval))
    plt.legend(loc='center left', bbox_to_anchor=(1, 0.5))
